# Formula One Data Analysis & Statistics Project
## Introduction
Welcome to the Formula One Data Analysis Project! This project is a comprehensive exploration of data spanning the past 150 years of Formula One racing. The primary goal is to extract actionable insights from historical data, employing statistical and probability analysis to determine the best-performing teams and players over the years.

## Project Overview
## Part 1: Historical Data Analysis
In the first phase of the project, we delve into the rich history of Formula One racing, examining data from the past 150 years. The focus is on applying advanced statistical techniques and probability models to unearth valuable insights. This analysis aims to answer questions such as:

Which teams have consistently performed well over the years?
Who are the standout players in the history of Formula One?
Are there any patterns or trends that can be identified in the data?
By leveraging data-driven methodologies, we aim to gain a deeper understanding of the sport's evolution and identify key factors contributing to success.

## Part 2: Live Sentiment Analysis on Twitter
The second phase of the project involves real-time sentiment analysis using Twitter data. We aim to gauge the public's sentiments and preferences towards Formula One teams and players. By analyzing tweets related to Formula One, we seek to answer questions such as:

Which players and teams are currently the most popular among fans?
Are there any emerging trends or shifts in public sentiment?
How do social media sentiments correlate with historical performance data?
By incorporating live sentiment analysis, we aim to capture the dynamic and evolving nature of public opinion in the world of Formula One.
